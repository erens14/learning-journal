# Article Summary — Building a Security Strategy for AI

**Source:** The Hacker News  
**Author:** Austin O'Saben - Kaseya
**Link:** <https://thehackernews.com/expert-insights/2026/06/building-security-strategy-for-ai.html>

---

## Summary

The article discusses how organizations need to rethink their cybersecurity strategy as Artificial Intelligence (AI) becomes deeply integrated into business operations. Rather than treating AI as just another application, organizations should recognize that AI introduces new attack surfaces, including AI models, training data, APIs, prompts, and sensitive information processed by AI systems. Traditional security controls alone are no longer sufficient to protect these emerging assets.

A key message is that AI security should be incorporated into an organization's overall security program from the beginning. This includes identifying AI assets, understanding how AI systems interact with existing infrastructure, applying governance, enforcing access controls, protecting data used for AI training and inference, and continuously monitoring AI-related activities. Security teams should also collaborate closely with AI developers and business stakeholders to ensure security is embedded throughout the AI lifecycle rather than added after deployment.

The article also highlights that AI creates risks beyond traditional cyber threats. Organizations must prepare for issues such as prompt injection, model manipulation, data leakage, insecure APIs, supply chain risks, and misuse of AI systems by both internal users and external attackers. Effective AI security therefore requires visibility into AI workloads, strong identity and access management, continuous monitoring, and governance policies that define how AI can be developed and used responsibly.

Ultimately, the article argues that AI security is becoming a core business requirement rather than a purely technical concern. As AI adoption accelerates, organizations that establish governance, secure architecture, continuous risk assessment, and cross-functional collaboration will be better positioned to benefit from AI while reducing security and compliance risks.

---

## Key Takeaways

* AI systems introduce new attack surfaces, including models, prompts, datasets, APIs, and AI pipelines.
* AI security should be integrated into the overall cybersecurity strategy rather than treated as a standalone initiative.
* Strong governance, identity management, and continuous monitoring are essential for protecting AI environments.
* Security should be incorporated throughout the entire AI lifecycle, from development to deployment and ongoing operations.
* Collaboration between security teams, AI engineers, and business stakeholders is critical for building secure AI solutions.

---

## Lesson Learned

* Traditional cybersecurity controls alone are not sufficient for protecting AI systems.
* AI security requires both technical safeguards and organizational governance.
* Securing AI involves protecting not only infrastructure but also data, models, prompts, and user interactions.
* A proactive security-by-design approach is more effective than attempting to secure AI after deployment.
